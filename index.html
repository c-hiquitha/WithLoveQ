Do you remember I once said something along the lines of “kamu kenapa masih lama banget sih ultahnya? Padahal aku udah mikir mau ngasih kado apa aja”? I’ve been thinking about writing you a birthday/love letter that looks like a Wikipedia page since long ago (I have it written down on my Google Keep Notes since March). So this is it.

Happy Birthday

Happy birthday, Za. Happy birthday to the brightest, friendliest, most ambitious, and of course, the hottest nerd I know. This is the hardest part to write since I don’t really know what to wish you: I know you don’t need wishes. You are someone with dreams and plans. You dare to dream big but you take steps small enough to realize it day by day (and that’s one of the things that make me fall deeper in love with you).

You are the most lively person and I don’t mean it like you live like there’s no tomorrow, you live like tomorrow is guaranteed for you. It seems like you fall in love with being alive and you do love life itself—though we might get into another debate on whether or not it’s too late to save humanity and whether or not life is worth all the suffering—that’s another thing I adore from you.

I hope you like the present I gave you. I know the paper is not so smooth and in ugly yellow shade but I hope the fact that the cover is illustrated by Pinot would make up for it.

Against All the Odds

Zaza, I don’t know how far we’ll go; I don’t know how many more birthdays we got to celebrate together, I don’t know how many more 26s of the month we got to see together, but I love to imagine that one day, we will sit on our yacht, sailing the peaceful and warm Mediterranean sea, and look back to this day only to say “We finally made it.”

We’ve beaten the odds so many times: Bumble (how many people do you know actually got a partner from a dating app?), Jogja (We went to Jogja? For a week? Crazy), finally made it to Bandung (KKN menjemput jodoh came true?) and don’t forget our so many discreet dates. So let’s do it again, let’s beat the odds for so many more times until we don’t have to anymore. Za, I will say it again in this letter: I believe in you, I believe in us.

Just like the song that played when we both finally confessed our love for the first time; we don’t know how long it will last and how will it end, we only know that we like each other, and truly, that’s the only thing that matters. (The song is On Se Plaît by Françoise Hardy, btw, just in case you don’t remember.)

Once again, happy birthday, Za. I’m looking forward to many more years of love, dreams, laughter, and birthdays with you. I love you.

With so much love,

Q
